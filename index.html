Where I Found Home

15 June 2026

It all started when I randomly watched your video. I instantly fell in love with the warmth of your smiles. I just wanted to know your names. Now it's all history. They say everything has a time, and you came perfectly on time to save me. Suddenly, I was not alone crying in the room. You were there through your music and words. I used to hate myself and was sad. You taught me how to love myself, slowly and gradually. You taught me how to smile. For you, I'd go every mile. Back home, I never thought I would get the opportunity to see you in person, that you would be closer to me than the screen, that I would see you smile in real life. Thank you for giving me the strength to never give up. Your music, your journey, and your words all give me encouragement through and through, telling me to keep trying, no matter what. No matter how much I thank you, it will never be enough.

You Knew

19 March 2026

You knew exactly what you were doing. Acted like it was unintentional, but you knew deep down how you treated me was shit. You knew I hated myself, so you did what you had to do. You made sure I hated myself more. Making fun of me was a daily routine for you. I would let it go, thinking this is how you were. But no, that wasn't the case. You appreciated everyone but me. I thought you did not know how to compliment someone, but you never let your friends down, complimenting them every chance you got. It made me think, how ugly could I be? I hated how I looked since a long time ago. You made it worse. I have never hated myself as much as I did when I was with you. Later, I realised I wasn't that ugly to be talked to like that. When people complimented me, I used to think they lied because you never said those things to me. Never to me. Now I know how nice it feels when people randomly compliment you. How being around people who don't make you feel like shit makes you a better person too. I thought I was so disgusting that you could never say a single nice word about me. But now I know I am not bad. I'll manage.

The Weight of Goodbye

29 April 2026

My heart was heavy that day. My parents had just gone away. I told you I was sad, but you had other plans because your day was bad. You were in pain and distress, so you found a way to make me feel worthless. I knew this day would come, but I did not realise I would already be numb. I genuinely do not remember anything about that day. How you had fights, so you projected it my way. I cried so much that it all became a blur, making my feelings and sadness defer. You were in pain, so you had to make me go in vain. I was the easiest thread to sever, so you made our connection disappear. I realised immediately that I lost a friendship. My life became a wrecked ship. I had no one to talk to, nobody to hold my hand and walk me through. Alone in a country with no friends, surrounded by nothing but pretends. I went through it all alone. I was never breakdown-prone. I somehow came out of it, but life shatters in a split. Suddenly, I cry. Hope I'll be okay by July. The attachment lingers, especially in lonely winters. I got used to talking to someone daily, that days feel empty lately. I am thankful for the memories, which were sweet, which, at one point, made me feel complete. I felt like I had someone, but now, again, it's back to none.

Genuinely I Don't Know

16 July 2026

I sit here and wait for them to ask, hoping they could discover the mask. Maybe someone could finally see that I just pretend to be full of glee. I was bursting with joy since the year began, wondering how my July would pan. Now that the most important event of my life is over, depression has taken over. I always make sure to check up on my friends, ensuring no loose ends. But when it's my turn, why would no one ask me if I feel free? I understand everyone has difficult days. It is a spiraling maze. But it just takes a text to see if I'm perplexed. I have always been alone, never letting the loneliness be known. I know this feeling won't always stay; I'll find my way, just not today.
